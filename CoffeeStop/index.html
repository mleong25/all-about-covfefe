Forget the Venti Frappaccino and Caramel Macchiatos - it's time to learn real coffee and where drinks are derived from so you know how to order and make these classics

Hot Coffees

Pour Over

A traditional, manual method that passes hot water over ground beans for a period of time in order to extract a smooth, subtle cup of coffee.
Clever

A nuanced emersion method that steeps water and grounds in a cone-inspired container for a bolder, flavorful cup of coffee.
Press

A sophisticated method of brewing that permeates water via plunging [pressing] to produce a purer flavor.
Siphon

A double chambered mechanism that creates a vacuum to use vapor pressure in order to produce a delicate, tea-like cup of coffee.

Cold Cofees

Cold Brew

Ice cold, refreshing coffee brewed with extended exposure of grounds and cold water to produce a smooth, less-acidic coffee beverage.

Nitro

Draft-like and rich in flavor, cold brew is conditioned with nitrogen for a creamier, cold coffee beverage.

Espresso-Based

Espresso

Robust, sharp shot extracted by forcing water through a packed amount of fine grounds using an espresso machine for a highly caffeinated per unit volume.

Americano

Coffee-resembling drink where espresso is diluted in water to have a similar strength but sharper flavor.

Latte

A 3:1 ratio of steamed milk to espresso for a delicate, balanced flavor often topped with equally aesthetic latte art.

Cappuccino

A 1:1:1 ratio of espresso, milk, and foam that cuts the acid from the espresso while preserving the flavor.

Macchiato

A bold shot of espresso sweetened with a dollop of milk foam which literally translates to "stained milk".

Cortado

Translated to "cut", espresso is 'cut' with steamed milk at usually a 1:1 or 1:2 ratio of espresso to milk.
